<title>987 Episode 59 What Ending (8)
Hyunsung wrapped his arms around Heewon's shoulder and hurriedly looked around. A pungent smell wafted through his nostrils. The sound of people evacuating. Intermittent screams. When was the last time he'd seen such a sight?
Hyunsung, unconsciously tense, called out to Heewon.
"Heewon-ssi."
To make matters worse, Heewon's condition was also strange. Normally, she wouldn't have suffered from dizziness or anything like this.
Tsutsutsu.
Hyunsung felt the story flowing through Heewon's body. A faint spark of probability flowed through the story. But that couldn't be the case. There were no scenarios in this worldline. There was no probability, no aftershocks.
<&>「A long time ago, Kim Dokja told me about this phenomenon.」
Hyunsung's eyes rippled violently once again. It felt like something long ago severed was reconnecting. Startled, Hyunsung grabbed Heewon's shoulder once more.
"Heewon-ssi?"
Heewon raised her head and swatted Hyunsung's hand away.
"Ah."
Hyunsung, meeting Heewon's eyes, involuntarily froze in his tracks.
The Heewon before him was undoubtedly the Heewon he knew. A longtime colleague who had worked together on scenarios. The woman who had swung her sword back to back beside him, a shield for the group. The person he wanted to protect more than anyone else.
But why? The Heewon before him now seemed like a different person.
Another explosion echoed from inside Geumho Station, where Wukong had rushed.
"Dokja-ssi."
Heewon muttered Kim Dokja's name and ran towards the station. Hyunsung, belatedly coming to his senses, also chased after her.

Wukong took a slow breath and looked around.
<&>「The 'scenario' hasn't begun yet.」
That meant the youngest hadn't truly desired that 'world' yet.
"You've managed to keep him hidden so well."
The interviewer, as usual, continued to ramble before his eyes.
"I've worked really hard to find him. To think that a world undetected even by the 'Recorder's' eyes exists is truly impressive."
"…"
"But even you can't stop the predestined [fate]."
Fate. Hearing that word for the first time in a long time, Wukong thought: Well, there's such a thing as fate.
"There is."
"There is. This entire world is being recorded, yet—"
"It's already been recorded. You seem to enjoy such tedious talk."
Wukong dug his pinky finger into his ear, seemingly bored. The interviewer frowned at him and said,
"This isn't the time to be taking it easy. I'm not the only one who discovered this world."
"So?"
"This means your time to hide him is over. Now, countless deities will come to this world line to find Kim Dokja."
"The youngest? Why?"
"Because this entire universe desires to complete 'One Perfect Dream'."
Wukong hesitated. A One Perfect Dream.
A long time ago, there was a time when Wukong, too, dreamed of something like that. It was a story of a time when he longed for ■■ more than anyone else.
"You too, wake up from your delusions. You no longer need to live protecting an incomplete fragment of a god."
"…"
"King of the Stone Monkeys, why are you still trapped in the past? Does seeing him remind you of your past? Are you projecting onto him all that you failed to protect?"
The interviewer took a step forward and spread his arms.
"Please join me. Stop being a prisoner of the golden headband. Free the imprisoned fragment of god."
"…"
"I know there's a reason to live in this imperfect delusion. But isn't this world truly the one you dreamed of? Great Sage, Heaven’s Equal, you are no longer a 'prisoner of the long-lost world'. Why did you confine yourself to this world with the stories you loved? There's no need. The dreams you desired can be recorded in an eternal record. If you help me—"
"I once saw someone talking like you at the Gourmet Association."
Cracking his neck, Wukong cut the interviewer off.
"You're such a petty brat, it took me a while to remember."
The interviewer's expression hardened subtly. Wukong added, as if mocking the interviewer.
"You're still a talkative as ever, Asmodeus."
A faint spark ran through the interviewer's body, his true name revealed.
"I didn't know you, a mere narrative-grade constellation, would become a 'Recorder.' Is that how you wanted to complete your ■■?"
After a brief pause, Asmodeus chuckled. Then, with a sigh, he asked back.
"How did you know it was me? You can't even use the power of stories."
"Few people have tongues as long as our youngest."
"That's a truly unpleasant compliment."
"So, what is your exact goal? You're taking our youngest to complete the record you desire, or something like that?"
"Judging by your tone, I don't think you intend to cooperate readily."
"Would you?"
"Then there's no need to argue further."
At Asmodeus's signal, black-suited men appeared behind him.
"Foolish Monkey King, this world is not the <Star Stream>."
The men charged in, overpowering Wukong's entire body. Crushed by the weight of the men, Wukong's head instantly slammed into the ground.
A world where neither stories nor skills could be used. Even for Wukong, it was physically impossible to escape four men twice his size.
Yet, with his head still on the ground, Wukong burst into laughter.
"Why are you laughing?"
"Because it's funny."
"Get the point. That's who you are now. You've cast aside all your dignity, and you're struggling in a paltry mortal shell. That's the reality of that proud, great, and powerful Heavenly King."
Asmodeus slowly approached, as if mocking the Monkey King trapped in the Five Elements Mountains. He bowed before Wukong, who was still head down, and whispered.
"I'll give you one last chance. Cooperate with me."
"You keep talking about cooperation…"
Wukong, still pinned to the ground, slowly raised his head and asked.
"It seems you've failed to persuade our youngest."
"…"
"Well, if that weren't the case, someone like you wouldn't be asking me for something like this."
"…"
"What did the youngest say? He wouldn't go anywhere without me, right?"
Asmodeus stared at Wukong with expressionless eyes for a moment, then slowly stood up. He stared into the darkness of the damp underground tunnel for a moment.
"This place is a perfect place for the Fallen Monkey King's tomb."
A young man waiting behind him pulled out a short knife. Asmodeus nodded.
The young man, having received the signal, was about to stab Wukong in the neck when...
"You've intruded into this world."
Wukong's laughter died down.
"Do you think you're the first?"
"What—"
Wukong sucked in a breath. He sucked and sucked. He continued to breathe, as if trying to suck in all the air around him, and the oxygen around him suddenly felt thin. Asmodeus, startled, took a step back and shouted,
"Kill him! Quickly!"
But the moment the young man plunged his knife into Wukong, Wukong's left arm escaped its bindings. His precisely extended fist swerved past the knife's trajectory and struck the young man's jaw. The young man's body swayed dully and fell to the floor.
"What…?"
Asmodeus frowned.
That couldn't be right. The young men he hired were all top-tier recruits, specially appointed by security agencies.
But Wukong, as if solving a simple puzzle, began to untie the young men's joints, carefully folding them.
"What, what—"
"Kwaaaaak! My arm!"
Asmodeus, unfazed, signaled again.
"Everyone, strike."
Following this, the young men waiting behind him charged in at once. Asmodeus had brought in additional personnel just in case.
Armed with stun guns and three-sectioned staffs, the men charged, surrounding Wukong from all sides. Even for Wukong, with his normal body, it was utterly insurmountable.
But Wukong, without a shred of tension, swung his fists dryly.
Thud, crunch!
The sounds of something exploding and breaking echoed in rapid succession. A dull, harsh violence.
After about a minute, only one man remained standing in the dark tunnel.
"Is this the end?"
In the pitch-black darkness, Wukong's eyes glowed a blazing gold.
Asmodeus shook his head in disbelief.
"How, in a worldline without a system—"
"Have you forgotten? I was always strong."
Only then did Asmodeus realize who the man before him was.
<&>「He is the Great Heavenly King. The great Monkey King who alone faced the <Emperor>.」
Wukong's shadow, striding swiftly toward him, fell over Asmodeus's head.
"I'm confident in fighting."
Only then did Asmodeus realize something was wrong, and he hurriedly turned his back to flee, but Wukong was faster. Asmodeus's knees and arms were instantly buckled against his opponent.
"Ugh."
Even Asmodeus couldn't hold back a groan under the swift yet brutal blow.
Wukong asked.
"Where's the youngest?"
Even in excruciating pain, Asmodeus smiled strangely.
"Still, you can't be stopped."
"Hmm, right? You haven't been hit enough yet?"
"Because he's calling all the Kim Dokjas from the 'Snowfield'."
At the mention of the snowfield, Wukong's expression stiffened for a moment.
Asmodeus continued.
"I asked you what security was. I'm really curious. How far you can protect him with that body…"
With those words, Asmodeus fell forward.
Wukong frowned and muttered.
"What is it, you punk?"
A faint vibration was felt at the tips of his feet. Then, a familiar smell reached his nose. Wukong suddenly raised his head and muttered.
"Youngest."
Wukong hurriedly ran toward the source of the smell. He must have run a few dozen meters through the tunnel. There he was, the youngest, unconscious and tied to the empty railroad tracks.
"Youngest, are you okay? I'm here! Your eldest brother is here!"
Kim Dokja remained silent.
"Hahaha, hahahahahaha—!"
Asmodeus's laughter was heard from somewhere. The vibrations felt in the ground were gradually getting stronger.
With an ominous premonition, Wukong frantically tried to untie the knot that bound Kim Dokja.
But it was so tightly bound, the knot wouldn't come off.
Asmodeus's laughter soon faded. The sound of flesh being torn, flesh being destroyed. A dazzling light illuminated the area.
<&>「It was the subway.」
A train, tearing through everything before his eyes, was hurtling towards them.
The youngest's body remained motionless, as if fused with the ground. He didn't bring a knife to untie the knot.
Great Sage, Heaven’s Equal stood up.
<&>「Can I?」
Watching the rushing subway, Wukong took another deep breath. His entire body twitched and swelled.
He couldn't use the power of stories, the power of stigma, or the power of his skills. All he had was a body slightly stronger than others. Could that alone be enough to stop that mass of steel? Before he knew it, the subway was right in front of him. Resolved, Wukong took his stance. You won't know until you try. If only I could somehow protect the youngest—
<&>「There's no way that'll work.」
He seemed to be hallucinating. Then, he felt someone shoving him hard. His vision spun around, and he was pushed to the edge of the tunnel. A bloody subway train passed before his eyes.
He blinked, not knowing what had happened, when a man stood beside him, gasping for breath.
"Youngest?"
The youngest had regained consciousness. Somehow, Kim Dokja, who had broken free of the knot in the floor, staggered to his feet and pushed him.
Steadying his pounding heart, Wukong examined the youngest.
But the moment he looked into the youngest's eyes, he knew something was wrong.
"You…"
He had been watching Kim Dokja longer than anyone, from the very moment the scenarios began. That's why he was certain. What was before his eyes was undoubtedly the youngest's body.
But,
"Who are you?"
The being inside the youngest's body was not the 'youngest' he knew.
